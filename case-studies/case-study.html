What was at stake

In compliance-heavy human services delivery, execution isn't just shipping work—it's protecting funding, safety, and trust. Work happens across sites with uneven capacity, and the proof of delivery is documentation quality.

When reporting is late or inconsistent, it becomes an operational risk: audits, corrective actions, delayed reimbursement, and avoidable client harm. The system had to increase coordination and visibility without adding process weight.

The operating system

The execution system is a set of lightweight rituals, artifacts, and views that make it easy to commit to a manageable slice of work, review outcomes frequently, surface dependencies early, and pivot predictably at sprint boundaries.

It's designed to run without a central hero PM—because continuity matters more than individual brilliance.

Dependency + risk mechanics

The system assumes change is normal—but it shouldn't be chaotic. Pivots happen at sprint boundaries unless a risk trigger requires immediate escalation. When dependencies slip or decisions stall, we surface the tradeoff early and document the decision so teams don't relive the same debate every sprint.

Pivot triggers

  • Dependency slips by >1 sprint
  • Critical decisions exceed 7 days
  • Capacity changes materially
  • External context shifts
  • Safety/legal/funding risk trigger hit
